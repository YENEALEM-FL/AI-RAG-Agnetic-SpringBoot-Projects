Leo was known as the “Bug Whisperer” in his team.
Whenever code misbehaved, everyone called him.
But Leo wasn’t just good at programming—he listened first, thought deeply, then typed quietly like he was talking politely to the machine.
One day, a system crashed right before launch.
Panic filled the room.
While others frantically clicked and rebooted, Leo calmly read logs, traced the issue, and fixed a tiny missing semicolon that caused chaos.
Cheers erupted. He smiled shyly and said,
“Good code is like good magic—quiet and precise.”
From that day, Leo became the mentor who taught others patience, clarity, and kindness in code.
